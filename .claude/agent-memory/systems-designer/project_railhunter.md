Railhunter is a turn-based mech RPG set on an abandoned research satellite in 2300. Solo-developed, 3-5 hour scope, $5-12 price point. Built in Godot 4.6.

Four pillars: (1) Exploration Density — every room must have a payoff; (2) Discovery > Numbers — new mechanics beat stat boosts; (3) Every Fight is a Build Test — weapon × ammo combos are the core depth; (4) Truth as Collection — narrative is assembled from collected fragments, not told.

Combat is dual-mode (manual + auto, switchable mid-fight). Player has 4 mech parts (head/chest/arm/leg), each with independent HP. ~20-30 weapons, 10-15 enemies across 3 chapters.

Why: solo + short scope means every system has to justify its existence. Pillar 2 ("Discovery > Numbers") is the one most likely to be violated by careless balance work — a +5% damage weapon with no new mechanic should not exist.

How to apply: when reviewing tuning, always check whether the change produces a "new discovery moment" or just a "bigger number." The latter is a pillar violation. Linked: [[feedback-shows-work-pattern]] for the review format.
